Bulbycode's Core Purpose

Digital Inclusion:
A Right, Not a Privilege.

In a world driven by connectivity, no person or business should be left behind. Digital inclusion is the foundation of economic empowerment, and it's the reason Bulbycode exists.

What is Digital Inclusion?

Digital inclusion refers to the equitable access to and meaningful use of digital technologies, including the internet, devices, software, and digital skills for all people regardless of their income, location, age, education level, or background.

It goes beyond simply having a smartphone or internet connection. True digital inclusion means having the skills to use technology effectively, the confidence to navigate digital platforms, and the access to affordable, reliable tools that allow people to participate in the modern economy.

In South Africa, where vast socioeconomic disparities exist between urban and rural areas, and between large corporates and informal traders, digital inclusion is one of the most urgent and impactful challenges of our time.

The Five Pillars of Digital Inclusion

Access to Connectivity

Broadband and mobile internet access must be affordable and available to all communities, urban, peri-urban, and rural. Without connectivity, digital participation is impossible.

Access to Devices

People need access to suitable devices, smartphones, tablets, laptops to engage with digital platforms. High device costs remain a major barrier in low-income communities.

Digital Literacy & Skills

Having access means nothing without the ability to use it. Digital literacy is understanding how to use the internet, software, and digital tools effectively and it is a foundational skill for the modern world.

Safety & Trust Online

Cybersecurity awareness and safe online practices are essential. People need to feel safe online to engage fully, especially small businesses handling transactions and customer data.

Relevant Local Content

Digital platforms must offer content and services in local languages and contexts relevant to South African communities, making technology genuinely useful and relatable.

Economic Participation

Ultimately, digital inclusion must translate into economic opportunity, jobs, business growth, access to markets, and financial services that improve livelihoods and build wealth.

Why the Digital Divide is an Urgent Problem

Economic Exclusion

Small businesses without a digital presence miss out on e-commerce markets, online advertising, and customer discovery. In a world where consumers search online first, invisibility equals lost revenue.

Skills Gap

The rapid digitisation of industries means workers and business owners without digital skills are at growing risk of being left behind. This disproportionately affects marginalised communities.

Limited Access to Finance

Digital financial tools - mobile banking, payment gateways, and fintech lending are transforming how businesses access capital. Those without digital infrastructure are excluded from these opportunities.

Post-Pandemic Reality

COVID-19 accelerated the shift to online business. Businesses without a digital presence suffered most. The future is digital and preparation starts now.

Youth Unemployment

South Africa has one of the highest youth unemployment rates in the world. Digital skills training and digitally empowered small businesses create new employment opportunities for young South Africans.
